Project Overview

Franco Manca is a pioneering sourdough pizza in the UK and was named the UK's best Italian Restaurant on Yelp.

Sadly, most users commented their app "never works" or "it's useless". The project goal is to identify usability issues and enhance user engagement and satisfaction for the Franco Manca app.

Usability test

Having the secondary research in mind, I conducted moderated usability testing to find how users interact with the current app and their pain points.

The researcher requested five participants to complete tasks on the app to observe user behaviour and feelings. The target audience is two males and three females 20-50 years old who are familiar with Franco Manca.

Competitive research

Understanding the market and competitor business deeper, I conducted a competitive audit to get direction on gaps and opportunities to address with the Franco Manca app.

The audit includes analysing their patterns and user flows to distinguish their strengths and weaknesses.

Identify issue areas with UX audit

I employed ten usability heuristics by Jakob Nielsen to find whether the design ticks all the usability boxes.

It's crucial to detect key problem areas with the existing interface. I found 7 improvement opportunities:

Develop

SCAMPER

1

Safer registration experience: Require only essential user details during the registration process. Also, supply alternative socials login for a choice.

2

Better issue handling experience: Improving the error copy for the virtual royalty card that includes "contacting us" as one solution.

3

Merge the features: Combining order & collect and the menu supplies a smoother effortless order experience. Also, stick an order through the app instead of jumping onto the company. Simplify the contents for a better user experience.

4

Enhance the UI design for better accessibility: typeface, typo, colour, touch targets, microcopy.

5

Clear navigation: Replace the complex hamburger and bottom menu bars with a concise bottom navigation bar.

Revised Information architecture

The original information architecture is convoluted that users struggle to find the contents they want immediately. I clarified the IA with fewer menu contents that we should robustly focus on business tracking as a priority.

Conclusion

Responsive Design:

It was my first redesign project and a fun problem to tackle. My main challenges were enhancing the usability and accessibility of the app to improve customer satisfaction without violating the brand image. Also, I attempted the subtle content evolution compared to the original design that minimises user alienation for the existing users.

Accessibility

There were some accessibility considerations to support a better user experience.

Low readability

Written in capital letters.

The original typeface is unique but illegible. Improved with clean typo styles.

Colour contrast lacks

Some icons are at the out-of-the-screen controls.

Small touch targets

Current contrast ratio is slightly under the WCAG standard (Improved from 4.11 to 6.35 AA).
